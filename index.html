About me

I'm a backend and systems engineer who's spent the last five years close to the infrastructure — writing Go services that move money across blockchain networks, building ML pipelines that recommend where to optimise cashback across millions of transactions, and architecting the event-driven systems that tie them together.

Before Spydra, I was at FireEye (now Trellix) building concurrent notification infrastructure that processed 10,000+ security alerts per second. Before that, I spent two years at IIIT Allahabad researching SCADA security, which ended up as a published paper in Springer's Networks and Systems series.

What I care about most: systems that fail gracefully, code that a future engineer will understand, and the unglamorous work of making something actually reliable — not just fast on a demo. Outside work, I built Engineering OS, an open learning platform with deep-dive courses on system design, distributed systems, ML, security, and DSA. It's the resource I wish had existed when I was getting started.

Currently based in Hyderabad. Open to remote, hybrid, or on-site work across Hyderabad, Noida, and Greater Noida.
